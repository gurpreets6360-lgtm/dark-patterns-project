Understanding Dark Patterns

How websites manipulate user choices — and how to spot them

Common Dark Patterns

Confirmshaming

Guilt-based manipulation

Newsletter Subscription

🔍 Analysis: The second option uses guilt to pressure users into subscribing.

Impact: Users feel ashamed to opt out.

✅ Solution: Neutral language like "No thanks" or "Unsubscribe."

Roach Motel

Easy in, hard out

Cancel Membership

Step 1: Click "Account Settings"
Step 2: Scroll to bottom → "Cancel Subscription"
Step 3: Click 3 confirmation screens
Step 4: Guilt message "We’re sad to see you go"

Impact: Users give up, leading to unwanted charges.

✅ Solution: One-click cancellation.

Nagging

Persistent interruptions

📱 Enable notifications? Get instant updates!

Impact: Repeated requests frustrate users.

✅ Solution: Respect user choices with "Don’t ask again".

✨ Ethical Design Principles

🔓 Clear Choices

Options presented equally without manipulation

🎯 No Manipulation

Avoid guilt, urgency, or hidden costs

🚪 Easy Unsubscribe

Cancel with same ease as signing up

❤️ Respect Users

Prioritize user autonomy over metrics
